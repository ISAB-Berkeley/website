Our Story

ISAB is the largest international students association at the UC Berkeley campus. It was established in 2009 and was nominated as an outstanding student organization in 2010.

ISAB provides a positive intellectual and social atmosphere for students from diverse backgrounds. We host numerous events to promote awareness of international culture to the campus community, whilst strengthening Berkeley's multicultural community.

Operations

The operations committee is comprised of the VPO, AVPO, Historian, and Tech Chair. They are responsible for keeping ISAB operations running smoothly behind the scenes, and taking great footage of it along the way!

External Events

The External Events Committee creates and organizes ISAB events that aim to reach out to the wider campus community. By organizing events like Pappy's Night, Color the Earth, World Food Fair and other large-scale events, we aim to connect with other clubs and organizations and therefore provide the platform for engagement with different cultures as an opportunity to soak in and experience the diversity on campus.

Marketing

The Marketing Committee is responsible for publicizing ISAB. This includes designing flyers/posters/tickets, having them printed, and creating tabling schedules. The Marketing Committee develops strategic, innovative, and creative marketing campaigns to advertise the large events that ISAB hosts, as well as managing and maintaining ISAB's facebook group and pages.

Collaborative Events

The Collaborative Events Committee strives towards nourishing the ISAB General Membership Program. From organizing various outings to managing the family system, you will have the chance to turn your crazily fun ideas into reality. Whether you like writing or managing events, fostering diversity or forming close relationships, join and let your creativity shine!

Social

The Social Committee aims to cultivate and develop relationships between core members within ISAB. Whether through arranging frequent social events or organizing our semesterly retreat to bring ISAB closer together, this committee should be comprised of people who can think on their feet and have the affinity to bring people together in any situation. The Social Committee needs people who love helping other people have a good time while sacrificing their own time to accomplish this goal. Responsibilities include planning eatouts, trips, kickbacks, a Banquet for the entire club at the end of the semester, and essentially to remind all of ISAB that it is always a priority to have fun.

International Programs

The committee's mission is to "act as a resource for international students to assist with any issues given their international student status in UC Berkeley, through events and programs in conjunction with BIO, ASUC and other organizations on campus." The International Program committee member's responsibilities will focus on assisting the Director in university relations. The member will contact Berkeley International Office, International Alumni Relations, ASUC, et cetera as well as sit in meetings with other official organizations and student clubs.
